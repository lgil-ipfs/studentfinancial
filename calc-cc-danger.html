The Minimum Payment Trap

Banks set low minimum payments (usually 3% of your balance) to keep you in debt as long as possible. High interest rates (like the standard 19.9% or 22.9%) mean that most of your minimum payment goes toward interest, leaving your balance virtually unchanged month after month.
